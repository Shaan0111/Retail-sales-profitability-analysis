# Findings & Recommendations

## Problem Statement

The objective of this project is to analyze retail sales data to understand profitability, product performance, and the impact of discounts, and to provide data-driven recommendations for improving business performance.

---

## 1. Profitability Problem

### Insight

Despite generating very high sales, the overall profit is extremely low.

* Accessories: ₹2.11B revenue, ₹268K profit (~0.01%)
* Electronics: ₹2.10B revenue, ₹339K profit (~0.02%)
* Fashion: ₹2.10B revenue, ₹525K profit (~0.02%)

Even after crossing ₹2 billion in revenue per category, the business retains almost no profit. This indicates a systemic issue, likely due to aggressive discounting or weak pricing strategies across all categories.

---

## 2. Discount Strategy Problem

### Insight

Discounting has a direct negative impact on profitability.

* Discounts below 10% → ~₹6,000 profit per order
* Discounts between 10–20% → profit becomes negative
* Discounts between 20–30% → ~₹6,500 loss per order

Nearly **48% of orders are loss-making**, contributing to an estimated loss of **₹215 million**. This shows that discounting is not just reducing profit but actively causing financial losses.

---

## 3. Product Performance Problem

### Insight

All products generate similar revenue (~₹1.2B–₹1.28B), but profitability varies significantly.

* Shoes: Loss of over ₹600K despite high sales
* Shirt: Loss-making despite strong revenue
* Laptop: Most profitable product with strong revenue and high profit

This indicates that high sales do not necessarily translate into profitability. Some products are negatively impacting overall business performance.

### Recommendation

* Focus on high-profit products like Laptop
* Re-evaluate pricing and discount strategies for Shoes and Shirt
* Reduce discounts on loss-making products
* Promote high-margin products instead of only high-selling ones

---

## 4. City Performance Problem

### Insight

Sales are similar across cities (~₹1.24B–₹1.29B), but profitability differs significantly.

* Delhi: Highest profit (~₹1.1M)
* Mumbai: Loss of ~₹361K despite strong sales
* Chennai: Highest revenue but negligible profit

This highlights that revenue alone is not a reliable performance indicator. Some cities are profitable, while others are generating losses.

### Recommendation

* Focus on Delhi as a high-performing market
* Investigate Mumbai to address losses (pricing/discount issues)
* Optimize strategy in Chennai to improve margins
* Implement city-specific pricing and discount strategies

---

## 5. Profit Margin Problem

### Insight

Revenue is evenly distributed, but profit margins vary significantly across products and categories.

* High-performing products:

  * Laptop (Electronics): ~₹1.19M profit
  * Bag (Fashion): ~₹940K profit
  * Shirt (Accessories): ~₹789K profit

* Loss-making products:

  * Shoes across multiple categories
  * Watch and Bag (Electronics)

This shows inconsistent profitability and lack of margin-focused decision-making.

---

## 6. High Discount Risk Problem

### Insight

A significant number of transactions involve high discounts, leading to losses.

* Orders with 20–30% discount frequently result in losses
* Some transactions lose over ₹10,000 per order
* Nearly 48% of orders are loss-making, largely driven by high discounts

This indicates that discounting is not controlled and is a major financial risk.

---

## Overall Recommendation

* Limit discounts to ≤10% to maintain profitability
* Shift focus from revenue to profit margin optimization
* Monitor product, category, and city-level profitability regularly
* Reduce or eliminate loss-making products or adjust their pricing
* Implement controlled and targeted discount strategies
* Track high-risk transactions (high discount + loss)

---

## Conclusion

The business generates strong revenue but fails to convert it into meaningful profit. The primary issue lies in uncontrolled discounting and lack of margin-focused strategies.

By improving pricing, controlling discounts, and focusing on profitability, the company can significantly enhance its financial performance without increasing sales volume.
